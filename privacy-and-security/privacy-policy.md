## 3. What personal data we collect

We collect personal data in three ways: data you give us directly, data generated by your use of the platform, and data from third parties.

### 3.1 Data you provide directly

**Account data (all users):**
- First name, last name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Role (client, business owner, or employee)
- Profile photo (optional)

**Business data (business owners):**
- Business name
- Business category
- Business description
- Business street address, city, province, and postal code, which you enter at signup
- Location (latitude and longitude). If you do not supply coordinates yourself, we send the address you entered to the Google Geocoding API to resolve them (see [subprocessors.md](subprocessors.md)) and store the result together with a marker recording how it was resolved.
- Service radius
- Business license information (when applying for Verified status)

**Employee data:**
- Name, email, phone (linked to user account)
- Job title within the business

**Service posts (clients):**
- Job title and description
- Category of service requested
- Budget range
- Location (for matching with nearby businesses)

**Booking and transaction data:**
- Booking details (date, service description, quoted price)
- Payment information — we do not store card numbers. Payments are processed by Stripe. See Section 8.

**Messages:**
- Text content of messages sent between clients and businesses. Messaging begins on a quote/interest thread once a business expresses interest in a job (before a booking is confirmed) and continues on the booking once the client accepts.

**Reviews:**
- Rating (1-5 stars) and written comment

**Cancellations:**
- Reason for cancellation, penalty amounts if applicable

**Disputes:**
- The issue type, description, and any resolution notes when a client or business opens a dispute about a booking

**Referrals:**
- Your referral code, and the link between a referring and referred account when a referral is used

**Account credits:**
- Credits granted to or redeemed by your account, and the reason and related booking

### 3.2 Data generated by your use of the platform

- Log data: IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, pages visited, time and date of access
- Location data: approximate location used for geo-browse and post matching (only when you permit location access)
- Usage data: features used, buttons clicked, time in app, screens viewed
- Error and crash data collected via Sentry (see [subprocessors.md](subprocessors.md))
- Push notification tokens: a device identifier used to deliver push notifications, if you enable them
- Administrative audit records: when a SwingBy administrator takes a privileged action affecting your account (suspending or reinstating it, force-completing a booking, or resolving a dispute), we record who acted, what changed, and when

### 3.3 Data from third parties

- Authentication data: if you sign in via a third-party provider (e.g., Apple, Google), we receive your name and email from that provider
- Payment confirmation data from Stripe (booking amounts, transaction IDs — not card numbers)

## 6. Who we share your data with

We do not sell your personal data. We share data only as needed to operate the platform.

**Within the platform:**
- When a business expresses interest in your post, their business name and profile are shared with you.
- **Before you accept — what a business can see.** While your job post is open, businesses browsing or quoting on it see the job title, description, category, budget, any photos you attached, your **first name and profile photo only**, and an **approximate location** (your address truncated to its city/province portion — the street number and street name are removed). Your last name and full street address are not shared at this stage.
- **After you accept.** When you accept a business's interest, a booking is created and your full name, full address, and contact details are shared with that business so the job can be completed.
- Employees can see booking details for bookings they are assigned to.
- **Employee visibility:** if you are added as an active employee of a business, your first name, last name, and profile photo are shown on that business's profile as part of its team roster, so that clients can see who may perform their service. This information is visible to any signed-in user viewing the business.

**Third-party service providers (subprocessors):**
We share data with service providers who help us operate the platform. All subprocessors are contractually required to protect your data and use it only for the specific purpose we've authorized. See the full list at [subprocessors.md](subprocessors.md).

Key subprocessors include:
- **Supabase** — database, authentication, and storage hosting (Canada, ca-central-1 region)
- **Stripe** — payment processing
- **Google Maps Platform** — map display and geocoding (we send business and job addresses to Google's Geocoding API to resolve coordinates)
- **Render** — hosting for the SwingBy API (United States); all API requests, including the personal data in them, transit Render
- **Sentry** — error monitoring (may include partial device/usage data)
- **Resend** — transactional email delivery
- **Expo** — mobile push notification delivery
- **Cloudflare** — DNS, CDN, and the waitlist endpoint
- **Plausible Analytics** — cookie-free product and site analytics
- **hCaptcha** — bot protection on signup
- **Notion** — storage of pre-launch waitlist sign-ups (name and email only)

**Legal requirements:**
We may disclose personal data if required to do so by law, court order, or government authority. We will notify you where legally permitted.

**Business transfers:**
In the event of a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, personal data may be transferred. We will notify you via email or prominent notice on the platform before your data is subject to a different privacy policy.

## 7. Data retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active and for a reasonable period after. Specific retention periods:

| Data type | Retention period |
|---|---|
| Account data | On deletion, your profile is deactivated and personal identifiers (name, email, phone, photo) are scrubbed or anonymized promptly. Records that must be retained for legal reasons (see below) are kept in anonymized/de-identified form. |
| Booking and payment records | **6 years from the end of the tax year they relate to** (Canada Revenue Agency financial-record requirement — see note below), even after account deletion, retained without your profile identifiers where possible |
| Messages | 2 years after the booking is completed or cancelled |
| Reviews | Until account deletion (or as long as the reviewed party has an account) |
| Logs and analytics | 90 days rolling |
| Crash/error data | 30 days rolling |
| Marketing email consent records | Until consent is withdrawn + 2 years |
| Administrative audit records | 24 months |

After retention periods expire, data is deleted or anonymized so it cannot be linked to an individual.

> **Note on the 6-year figure.** Canada's *Income Tax Act* s.230(4) and *Excise
> Tax Act* s.286 both require books and records to be kept for **six years from
> the end of the last taxation year to which they relate** — not six years from
> the transaction date. In practice a January transaction is therefore held for
> close to seven calendar years. Earlier drafts of these documents said
> "7 years", which is the United States norm and is not the Canadian statutory
> rule; every SwingBy document now states the statutory six-year rule with the
> "end of the tax year" qualifier, which is what the code implements
> (`backend/app/api/me.py`).

## 9. User rights

You have rights over your personal data. To exercise any of them, email privacy@swingbyy.com with "Privacy Request" in the subject line. We will respond within 30 days (PIPEDA) or 30 days (GDPR).

### 9.1 Right to access

You can request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.

### 9.2 Right to correction

You can request that inaccurate or incomplete data be corrected. You can also update most account data directly in the app under Settings → Profile.

### 9.3 Right to deletion

You can delete your account and personal data. Because SwingBy handles payments, some records are subject to legal retention (see Section 7), so "deletion" means we deactivate your account and scrub or anonymize your personal identifiers — not that we erase financial records we are legally required to keep.

**How to delete:**
- **Permanent deletion** requires you to re-enter your current password to confirm. Once confirmed, your account is deactivated, you are signed out and blocked from signing in again, and your name, email address, phone number, and profile photo are scrubbed or replaced with a non-reversible placeholder. Financial and relational records (bookings, payments, invoices, cancellations, reviews, messages, and any business you own) are retained in de-identified form for the period in Section 7.
- **In-app "ghost mode"** is a reversible alternative available in the mobile app. It hides your profile from search, the map, and matching, and makes you unbookable, while keeping your existing bookings and message threads intact. You cannot enter ghost mode while you have an active booking, money still held in escrow, or an open dispute.

### 9.4 Right to portability

You can export your data in a machine-readable format (JSON) at any time from within the app, or by emailing privacy@swingbyy.com. The export covers your account record, any businesses you own, your job posts, quotes/interests, bookings, payments, messages, reviews, and cancellations.

### 9.5 Right to object (GDPR)

If you are an EU user, you have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests.

### 9.6 Right to opt out (CCPA)

California residents have the right to opt out of the "sale" of personal information. We do not sell personal information. California residents may also request disclosure of categories of data collected and shared in the past 12 months.

### 9.7 Right to withdraw consent

You can withdraw consent at any time by emailing privacy@swingbyy.com. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

## 12. Governing privacy law and international data transfers

### 12.1 Which laws apply

SwingBy operates from Calgary, Alberta. The laws that govern this policy are Canada's federal **Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)** and **Alberta's Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA)**, which the federal government has declared substantially similar to PIPEDA for commercial activity within Alberta.

SwingBy does not currently operate in, market to, or hold a place of business in Quebec, so Quebec's *Law 25* is not treated as a governing regime in this policy. Because Law 25 attaches to collecting personal information about people **in** Quebec regardless of where the business sits, this must be re-assessed before SwingBy accepts users in Quebec — see `COMPLIANCE-REGISTER.md`.

The GDPR and CCPA sections of this policy (§9.5, §9.6) are written defensively for the case where EU or California residents sign up; they are not an assertion that SwingBy currently targets those markets.

### 12.2 Where data goes

SwingBy's primary database is hosted by Supabase in the ca-central-1 region (AWS Canada, Montreal). Personal data at rest is stored in Canada by default.

However, the SwingBy API itself is hosted in the **United States** (Render), which means personal data submitted through the app transits and is processed in the United States before it is stored in Canada. Other subprocessors also process data outside Canada (Stripe, Sentry, Google, Expo, Resend, Cloudflare's global edge). While personal data is outside Canada it is subject to the laws of that jurisdiction, including lawful access by foreign courts and authorities. Where transfers occur we rely on contractual data-protection terms (standard contractual clauses or each vendor's equivalent).
