# Skill: internal-focus-group

> Borrowed from Austin Marchese's "6 Skills." Simulate your real target users reacting to a screen or flow BEFORE you ship it — catch confusion and friction without waiting for real testers.

## When it triggers
Before shipping any user-facing screen or flow (signup, post-a-job, quote, booking, Live Job Status, etc.).

## The panel (SwingBy's actual users)
1. **Dav, 38 — Calgary tradesperson** (busy, not techy, on his phone in a truck). Cares: fast, fair, gets paid, no haggling.
2. **Priya, 34 — busy homeowner** (wants it done, trusts cautiously, hates surprises). Cares: will they show up? is my money safe? proof it's done?
3. **Skeptic — first-time user** who's been burned by a no-show before. Cares: why should I trust this over calling a guy?

## The move
1. Walk each persona through the screen/flow step by step.
2. Each reports: **where they hesitated, what confused them, what would make them drop off, the one thing that would win their trust.**
3. Collect the top 3 friction points → fix before ship.
4. Tie back to the moat: does this screen make trust *visible* (Live Job Status, before/after, escrow)?

## Why it fits Kira
She's pre-beta with no real users yet. This is a free, instant proxy — it makes the UX professional and trust-forward before a single real tester sees it, so the beta lands.
