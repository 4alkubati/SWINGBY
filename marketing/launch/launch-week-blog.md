# Why I Built SwingBy — Founder Blog Post

**Target:** SwingBy blog, published Day 1

**Tone:** Personal, honest, no hype. Reads like a letter, not a press release.

---

## Why I Built SwingBy

There's a moment every founder has — the one they point back to when someone asks "why did you build this?"

Mine happened on a Tuesday afternoon. I'd hired a contractor off one of those local services platforms to fix a bathroom fixture. He seemed legitimate: a few reviews, reasonable price, confirmed the appointment. I sent him a deposit through the platform.

He never showed up.

I called the platform. Their response was essentially: "We're a listing service, not a contractor. We're not responsible for third-party transactions."

I lost $300. But more than that — I lost two weeks of rescheduling, waiting, and starting over. The platform kept the booking fee. The contractor kept the deposit. I got nothing.

And I thought: this is the model that's "working" for local services?

---

### The problem is trust, not tech

The local services market in Canada is massive — tens of billions of dollars a year in transactions. Home repair, cleaning, landscaping, moving, personal training, tutoring — almost every adult in every city needs these services regularly.

And yet the best discovery tool most people have is: ask a friend, check Kijiji, hope for the best.

When I started looking at existing platforms, I noticed something. They'd solved the discovery problem — you can find a business — but they hadn't solved the trust problem. The reviews are gameable. The payment isn't protected. The platform has no skin in the game. If a business doesn't show up or does bad work, the platform's response is usually some variation of "we're sorry to hear that."

That's not a marketplace. That's a phone book with a booking button.

---

### The escrow insight

The moment I landed on the escrow model, everything clicked.

Here's the idea: when a client books a job on SwingBy, their payment is held in escrow immediately. The business gets paid in two tranches — 50% when the booking is confirmed, 50% when the job is marked complete.

This does three things:

**It protects clients.** The client's money doesn't go anywhere until the work is done. A contractor can't disappear with a deposit because the deposit is sitting in escrow.

**It creates accountability for businesses.** If a business no-shows or does shoddy work, they don't get their second tranche. The money doesn't automatically flow to them — it waits for completion.

**It builds trust by design.** You don't have to hope that the business is trustworthy. The system makes trustworthy behavior the path of least resistance.

I know escrow isn't a new idea. But combining it with a local discovery and bidding system — that's the gap I didn't see anyone filling in Calgary.

---

### Why Calgary

I live here. That's the honest answer.

But it's also the right answer for a marketplace. Local services are intensely local — what works in Toronto doesn't automatically work in Calgary. The categories are different, the pricing is different, the culture is different.

Calgary has a large base of skilled tradespeople and service businesses. It has neighbourhoods where everyone knows their street but not the contractor two blocks over. It has homeowners who want to buy local but don't know who's actually good.

And it has a startup scene that's slowly realizing it doesn't have to let Toronto or Vancouver define what a "Canadian tech company" looks like.

We're starting here. We'll go further when the time is right.

---

### What we actually built

SwingBy has two sides.

**For clients:** You post a job. You describe what you need, set a budget, and the system shows it to local businesses that match your location and service category. Businesses that are interested send you a quote. You review who's reached out, check their profile and reviews, and choose who you want. Once you accept a quote, a booking is created, payment is confirmed in escrow, and you're connected through the app for the logistics.

**For businesses:** You set up your profile — what you do, where you serve, what your average job looks like. You browse open jobs near you. You send quotes to the ones that fit. When a client accepts your quote, you get 50% upfront (from escrow) and the rest when you complete the job. You never have to cold-call or wonder where your next customer is coming from.

There's no monthly fee. No subscription. SwingBy takes a 10% platform cut from completed jobs — so we only make money when you do.

---

### What we don't have yet

I want to be honest about this.

We're launching without Stripe fully integrated. Payments in the first version are handled manually — I'll facilitate the first few transactions personally to make sure the escrow model works in practice before automating it.

We're also launching mobile-web first, not native app first. The React Native app is built; we're fine-tuning it before the App Store submission. For now, swingbyapp.com works great on mobile browsers.

And we're in Calgary only. Not Edmonton. Not Vancouver. Not Toronto. One city, done right, before we think about expansion.

This is the truth: day-one products are never finished. The question is whether they're useful. We believe this one is.

---

### The ask

If you're in Calgary, try it. Post a job — something you genuinely need done. Or if you run a service business, set up your profile and send a quote.

That's all a marketplace needs to prove itself: two sides showing up.

If you're not in Calgary: tell someone who is. Or just watch what we do. If it works here, it works everywhere.

And if you have thoughts — on the product, the model, the launch, what I'm missing — reply to this post. I'm not a company yet. I'm one person trying to do something useful. Feedback goes directly to me.

Thank you for reading. Let's see what happens.
