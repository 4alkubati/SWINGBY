# Launch Day Anti-Checklist — Things NOT To Do

Read this the night before launch. Read it again at 7 AM.

---

## Do NOT do these things on launch day

---

### Code & Tech

**❌ Do not ship new code on launch day unless something is broken.**
- The urge to "just fix this one small thing" will hit you at 10 AM. Resist it.
- If it's not broken, don't touch it. Every deploy is a risk. Save new features for day 2.

**❌ Do not push directly to production without testing.**
- Even the smallest change. Even a typo fix. Test it in staging first.

**❌ Do not skip the smoke test before going live.**
- Client signup → post job → business quote → acceptance → booking → messages. Walk through it yourself. Every step.

**❌ Do not ignore error logs.**
- Check Sentry (or whatever crash tool you use) starting at 7 AM. Know your baseline before users arrive.

**❌ Do not disable alerts "just for today."**
- Uptime alerts, error rate alerts — leave them on. You want to know the moment something breaks.

---

### Marketing & Comms

**❌ Do not post inconsistent pricing or features across channels.**
- If your email says one thing and your website says another, you'll spend launch day answering "which is right?"

**❌ Do not send the launch email and immediately send a follow-up.**
- Let the first email breathe. Wait at least 4 hours before any "did you see this?" follow-up.

**❌ Do not promise things you haven't built.**
- If a journalist or user asks "can it do X?" and you're not sure — say "not yet." Never imply yes to a feature that's still in progress.

**❌ Do not pitch journalists on launch day unless you've already connected.**
- Launch day cold pitches almost always get ignored. Do press outreach in the week before or after.

**❌ Do not post a public "Day 1 numbers" update until you have real numbers.**
- "We have thousands of signups" with no actual data is the kind of thing people screenshot and mock. Only post real, specific numbers.

---

### Mindset & Operations

**❌ Do not panic-react to every piece of feedback.**
- Someone will say your product is bad. Someone will say it's great. Neither one is complete truth. Observe patterns, not individual comments.

**❌ Do not stay up past midnight.**
- You need to be sharp on day 2. Nothing critical enough to lose sleep over will happen in the first 24 hours that can't wait until morning.

**❌ Do not check your phone obsessively during conversations.**
- If you're on a call or in person with someone today, be present. The dashboard will look the same in 30 minutes.

**❌ Do not compare your day-1 to someone else's day-1.**
- You don't know the full story behind any public launch number. Your launch is your launch.

**❌ Do not make major product decisions based on day-1 data.**
- One day is noise. If 10 people ask for the same thing, note it. Don't build it yet.

**❌ Do not quietly panic.**
- If something breaks and you don't know how to fix it — tell someone. Your co-founder, an advisor, a technical friend. Isolation makes problems worse. Talking makes them smaller.

**❌ Do not forget to eat.**
- Sounds dumb. You'll forget. Set an alarm for lunch.

---

## The one thing you MUST do

At 11:59 PM on launch day, write down three sentences:
1. What went better than expected?
2. What needs fixing tomorrow?
3. What are you grateful for?

Then close the laptop. You launched.

---

*Print this. Tape it next to your monitor. Cross things off as you successfully avoid them.*
