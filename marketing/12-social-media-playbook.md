# 12 — Social Media Playbook

> Day-to-day operating manual for SwingBy's social presence.

---

## Principles

1. **Be the most helpful account about local services in Calgary.** Not the most promotional. Not the funniest. The most useful.
2. **One platform at a time.** Get good at Instagram before adding TikTok. Add LinkedIn when you have 1,000 Instagram followers. Don't stretch thin.
3. **Respond within 2 hours.** Speed signals trust. Slow response = indifference.
4. **Show the humans behind the app.** The founder, the businesses, the clients. Not the logo.
5. **Post consistently.** Three times a week for six months beats ten times a week for three weeks.

---

## Platform-specific best practices

### Instagram

**Audience:** Calgary 22-45, home renters and owners, both clients and potential business owners.

**What performs:**
- Before/after photos (cleaning, painting, landscaping)
- 15-30 second reels — quick demos, day-in-the-life, founder clips
- Carousels: "5 things to ask before you hire a handyman"
- Business spotlights: name, photo, one quote

**What doesn't work:**
- Pure promotional posts ("Book now! Great services!")
- Stock photos
- Long-form captions on single images
- Anything that looks like an ad

**Profile setup:**
- Bio: "Calgary's service marketplace. Find a cleaner, handyman, dog walker. Free for clients."
- Link in bio: swingbyy.com or a Linktree with app download + website
- Highlight covers: How it Works / Businesses / Reviews / About

**Technical:**
- Post at 7-9am or 6-8pm MT (when Calgary audience is scrolling)
- Use location tags: Calgary, AB + specific neighbourhood
- Tag featured businesses in their spotlight posts

---

### TikTok

**Audience:** Younger side of the demo, 18-35. Heavy on Calgary lifestyle content.

**What performs:**
- Authentic, imperfect, 15-30s clips
- Founder narrating over screen recording of the app
- "Watch me do X in 60 seconds" format
- Behind-the-build content (founder building the product)
- Reaction to relatable problems: "When your cleaner ghosts you..."

**What doesn't work:**
- Highly produced video (looks like an ad)
- Anything over 60 seconds (unless hooks are strong)
- Using trending sounds that don't match the content

**Hook rules:** The first 2 seconds must make someone stop scrolling. Use text overlay on the opening frame. Start with a surprising fact or a relatable problem, not an introduction.

**Caption:** Short. 1-2 sentences max. 3-5 relevant hashtags: #Calgary #CalgaryServices #CleaningCalgary #SwingBy #LocalBusiness

**Posting time:** 6-9pm MT, Monday through Thursday.

---

### LinkedIn

**Audience:** Calgary business owners, professionals who hire services, potential business partners.

**What performs:**
- Founder personal posts (first-person, specific, honest)
- Wins and failures — people engage with authenticity
- Data posts: "We completed X bookings this week. Here's what we learned."
- Opinion pieces: "Why Calgary's service market is broken" (local angle)
- Behind-the-build: "What I shipped this week"

**What doesn't work:**
- Company page posts without founder engagement
- Generic inspirational quotes
- Press-release-style writing

**Two-account strategy:**
- **Company page** (@SwingBy): milestone announcements, job postings, partner announcements
- **Founder personal** (@Amr Alkubati or equivalent): everything real. This is where engagement lives.

Most founder personal posts should mention/tag the company page.

**Caption length:** 100-200 words. No headers. White space between paragraphs. No hashtags (they look desperate on LinkedIn).

---

### Twitter / X

**Audience:** Builders, local tech community, early adopters.

**What performs:**
- Short takes: "Two-sided marketplaces die on day one if supply isn't locked before demand arrives."
- Threads: "Here's what we did our first week live: [thread]"
- Build-in-public updates: "We hit [X] bookings today."
- Engagement with Calgary accounts

**What doesn't work:**
- Pure promotional tweets
- Posting the same content from Instagram
- Long promotional threads

**Cadence:** 5 posts/week. 1 thread per week. Fill the rest with quick takes and replies.

**Engagement rule:** Spend 15 minutes a day replying to tweets in the Calgary startup and local business space. Replies drive followers faster than original posts.

---

### Facebook

**Audience:** Slightly older, 30-55, home renters and owners, community-oriented.

**What performs:**
- Local community stories
- "Meet a local business" posts
- Event announcements (if SwingBy runs any)
- Sharing blog posts (Facebook still drives blog traffic)

**Where to post:**
- SwingBy company page
- Calgary community groups (as a human, not a brand — disclose you're the founder)

**Facebook groups to engage:**
- "Calgary Buy & Sell"
- "Calgary Home Improvement and Renovation"
- "Beltline Community" (and equivalent for other neighbourhoods)
- Neighbourhood-specific Facebook groups

**Rule:** Be a neighbour, not a marketer. Add value first. Mention SwingBy when relevant and disclose.

## Hashtag strategy per platform

### Instagram

**Category:** always use
- #Calgary #YYC #CalgaryBusiness #CalgaryLocal #yyc

**Service-specific:** rotate by post
- #CalgaryCleaningServices #CalgaryHandyman #CalgaryDogWalker #CalgaryMoving

**Niche:**
- #SwingBy #LocalServicesYYC #HireLocal #CalgaryMade

**Quantity:** 8-12 hashtags per post. Put them in the caption, not the comments.

### TikTok

3-5 hashtags only. Use category + location + SwingBy.
- #Calgary #SwingBy + 1-2 category-specific tags

### LinkedIn

None. Or at most 2-3 very general (#Calgary #Entrepreneurship). Hashtag stuffing on LinkedIn signals spam.

### Twitter / X

1-2 per tweet max. #Calgary and one relevant term. Don't use hashtags in threads.

### Facebook

None needed on the company page. In community groups, use 0 — they look promotional.

## Engagement playbook

### Responding to comments

**Rule:** Reply within 2 hours during business hours. Reply within 8 hours evenings and weekends.

**Format:**
- Acknowledge the specific thing they said
- Add something useful or human
- End with a question or a light call to action

**Good reply example:**
> @user_handle Thanks for sharing that — cleaning quotes vary a lot in Calgary depending on home size and frequency. The easiest way to get an accurate quote is to post a job on SwingBy — businesses will send you their actual prices. What size is your place?

**Bad reply example:**
> Thanks! Check out our app! 😊

---

### Responding to DMs

**Triage within 2 hours.**

| Type | What to do |
|---|---|
| Service inquiry | "Thanks for reaching out! The fastest way is to post a job at swingbyy.com — businesses will come to you with quotes. Takes 60 seconds." |
| "How much does X cost?" | Give a real answer with a Calgary range. Then offer the app as the way to get an exact quote. |
| Complaint | "I'm really sorry to hear that. Can you send me the details — booking ID or your email? I'll look into it personally." Never say "please contact support" without giving a name. |
| Job application | "Thanks for your interest! We're hiring [yes/no]. Apply at [link]." |
| Partnership inquiry | "Appreciate the note — I'm the founder. Can you send me an email at [email]? Happy to connect." |
| Spam | Mark as spam, do not reply. |

---

### Responding to mentions and tags

If someone tags SwingBy or mentions it without tagging:
1. Like the post immediately.
2. If it's positive: Comment with genuine thanks + share to Stories.
3. If it's negative: Reply publicly with empathy, take the conversation to DM.
4. If it's a question: Answer fully and helpfully.

---

### Escalation — when to involve the founder

Escalate immediately when:
- A complaint involves a payment dispute, no-show, or safety issue
- A negative post or comment is getting significant engagement (over 10 comments or shares)
- A journalist or media account reaches out
- Anyone mentions legal, police, or a formal complaint

---

## Crisis management

### Scenario 1 — Negative review going viral

**What "viral" means for us in year 1:** 100+ comments or shares on a negative post.

**Response protocol:**
1. Do not delete the comment (it makes it worse).
2. Reply publicly within 1 hour. Be direct: "We're aware of this. I'm looking into it personally. Can you DM me the details?" Sign with the founder's name.
3. DM the person: get the facts, offer a resolution (refund, credit, re-do the job).
4. Update the public comment thread: "We've spoken with [customer name — only if they consent]. Here's what we're doing."
5. If it involves a business on the platform and they behaved badly: share what action was taken (warning, suspension) without naming the business unless necessary.

**What not to do:**
- Do not argue publicly.
- Do not be defensive.
- Do not blame the client.
- Do not offer a public refund before you understand the situation.

---

### Scenario 2 — Service issue (no-show, damage, fraud)

**Internal:** Log the issue. Review the booking in the backend. Pull messages. Understand who is at fault.

**External:** Acknowledge on the platform where the complaint lives. Move to DM. Resolve the specific case. If it's a pattern (multiple no-shows by the same business), offboard the business.

**Post-resolution post (optional, if serious enough):** "We had a service issue last week. Here's how we handled it and what we're changing." This builds trust. Most companies hide failures — showing how you handle them builds credibility.

---

### Scenario 3 — Competitor attack or false claim

**Example:** A competitor's account posts "SwingBy charges 20% fees" (false).

**Response:**
- Reply with the correct fact: "Our fee is 10%, charged to the business only. Clients are always free. Here's our pricing page: [link]."
- Do not mention the competitor by name.
- Do not escalate unless it becomes persistent (then document and consult a lawyer).

---

### Scenario 4 — Data or security issue

**Do not post about it on social media before an internal review.** See [../privacy-and-security/incident-response.md](../privacy-and-security/incident-response.md) for the full protocol. Only communicate publicly after the issue is contained and legal/founder review is complete.
